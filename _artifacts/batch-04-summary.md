## Call 10: AI as Conversation Participant (Oct 9, 2025)

**Title**: "AI as Conversation Participant: Tools and Understanding"

**The Big Epiphany**: John Warinner's light bulb moment - everyone is having revealing one-on-one explorations with ChatGPT and trying to share 2,000-4,000 word transcripts. **"Why aren't LLMs just participants in THIS conversation?"** Asked ChatGPT how far off - response: couple years away, in the pipeline. Would be a different chapter than the current one-on-one era.

### Key Themes

**John Warinner's Reframe**: Changed the question from "How do we share AI conversations?" to "Why aren't AIs here with us?" This is a profound shift - instead of trying to solve the bandwidth problem of sharing transcripts, bring the AI into the room as a participant.

**Gene Bellinger's Wisdom Applied**: "The value is the gerund - it's the mapping, not the map." Applied to AI conversations: the value is in the conversation itself, not the product. Jerry notes that other people's maps are often impenetrable unless unpacked slowly - small percentage can navigate Jerry's Brain solo, most need guidance, few deeply understand WHY specific contexts were curated.

**Practical AI Tools Already Working**:
- **Stacey's podcast converter**: Ran long AI chat through LLM to create short podcast as "great beginning point for a conversation" - loved that it asked good questions
- **Jerry's anonymized focus group**: 60-70 people as "Orange Panda" (color + animal), broken into breakouts, AI summarizing each group and cross-fertilizing insights. "AI was being really helpful in ways I hadn't experienced before in that kind of setting. I look forward to having AIs participating as excellent listeners and summarizers."
- **Shawn Murphy's Thinkertoys**: Real-time collaborative knowledge tool - group edits LLM prompt together, run it, outputs in Turtle format (graph knowledge representation), immediately visualize it. Near real-time collaboration with AI populating shared workspace. Will invite OGM to try it.

**Enshittification and Monetization**: Gil Friend asks the deep question - "Is enshittification tightly correlated with monetization? How do you build something with appropriate reach that can be sustained economically without falling into enshittification machine?" Jerry: "Big question: is it possible to have viable, sustainable, ongoing concern software that doesn't need to go down the enshittification rat hole?"

**Why Are We Here?**:
- **Alex**: "Hear new perspectives from different people with different viewpoint. Makes me think."
- **Jerry**: "Learn stuff I would never figure out. Love who shows up, social interactions. Love that we know what we do - 4-5 years of history. Bringing people together who then do projects - 'if three people met and did something they loved, that's gold.' **FRUSTRATED desire: 'I'd really like to build a global mind... We haven't done much of that at all.'**"
- **Doug**: "Understanding - not agreement, just common understanding at semantic level. Helping with decision-making. LLMs enable handling complex array of variables in real-time like never before."

**Kevin Jones Returns**: After being absent entire Batch 3 (Calls 07-09), Kevin returned for Call 10 with minimal participation. Status unclear - one-time return or resuming?

### Emotional Tone
Excited about AI possibilities, wrestling with practical challenges, reflective on OGM's purpose and limitations, hopeful about tools being built.

## Call 12: Has Progress Stopped? (Oct 23, 2025)

**Title**: "Has Progress Stopped?"

**The Pattern Repeats**: Like Calls 05, 07, 08, 09, the group immediately fragments on the definition. Mike Nelson (self-describes as "provocative and obnoxious"): "We've been diverted 10 minutes defining progress, not useful, all have different definitions (Mike has 5+). Question so broad we'll all talk past each other."

### Key Themes

**Vested Interests Suppressing Progress** (Alex Kladitis):
- "Big interests now work AGAINST progress - exception is computing/IT"
- "Best battery ever invented tomorrow? Oil industry + governments would say 'too much economic impact' and buy it out"
- "Overweight hegemonic interests in world economy suppressing massive disruptive progress"
- Anticipated pushback: "You're a conspiracy theorist, Alex" - but thinks this is part of the cycle
- **Jerry's confirmation**: "Vested interests kill golden goose progress"
- **Historical example**: Great American Streetcar Conspiracy - GM, Firestone, Phillips66, Mack Truck bought light rail lines, turned down service, shut down lines, pulled up rails, burned cars → car culture not public transit

**What IS Progress?** (Gil Friend):
- "Question behind the question: What IS progress?"
- Examples across scales:
  - **Global**: Renewable energy > coal now, striking price/performance progress over 20-30 years
  - **Personal**: Wife 11 years in remission from disease that would've killed her in 2 years (cancer research progress)
  - **Home**: Induction range, rooftop solar, backup battery switching in microseconds - better experience, "same electrons"
- **Challenge**: "US lack of progress vs European cities shows progress may be highly localized, not about humanity overall"

**Mike's Narrowing Attempt**:
- "Rewrite the question: Are there more barriers to technological development than in the past?"
- Mike's 5+ progress dimensions:
  1. Scientific/research discoveries
  2. Applying discoveries in cool new technologies
  3. Economic barriers preventing use (super battery example)
  4. Building societies in better ways (societal progress, governance evolution)
- Alternate framing: "Quality of daily life improvement over 200 years plateauing or ceasing?"
- Mike's view: "Would argue against globally, but US quality of life not doing well - 'amusing ourselves to death', conflicting demands, **Confusopoly** (Dilbert term) - companies throwing so many options, constantly confusing what product is and how much we pay"

**Enshittification Theme Returns** (Mike Nelson):
- "Companies won't answer phone when you call them"
- "Despite new AI tools, call centers doing BETTER job of diverting away from end goal than getting there"
- Book recommendations:
  - **"Enshittification" by Cory Doctorow**: "Why can't we have good things? Why aren't tech breakthroughs leading to customer service + quality of life improvements?"
  - **"Breakneck"**: "Deeper and more important book"

**Progress as Transformation of Daily Life** (Jerry and Gil's surface metric):
- **Jerry's examples**: Electrification (grandparents), hygiene revolution (great-grandparents: outhouse → running clean water), supersonic travel would be perceived (vs renewable energy behind curtain), AI might be next large transformation
- **Gil's counter**: "Behind curtain matters too - rooftop solar, induction range, backup battery = better experience even if 'same electrons'"
- **Jerry's skepticism**: "Don't particularly want urban airscapes littered with drones/air taxis - fender bender ignorable, quadcopter crash not. Maybe real progress is NOT pushing addictive technology on us - progress might be entirely different."

### Questions Raised (Partial List)
- Has progress stopped?
- What is progress?
- Are there more barriers to technological development than in the past?
- Why do vested interests suppress disruptive progress?
- Is progress transformation of daily life, or deeper systemic change?
- Why aren't technological breakthroughs leading to quality of life improvements?
- Is progress localized (Europe vs US) rather than global?
- Where is my flying car?
- Is AI the next large transformation we're living through?

### Emotional Tone
Provocative (Mike self-describes), definitional wrestling, skepticism about mainstream progress narrative.
