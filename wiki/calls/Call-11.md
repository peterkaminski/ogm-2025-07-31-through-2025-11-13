---

### Cosplay Diplomacy

**Mike Nelson's critique of Trump's diplomatic claims**

**Concept**: Claims of stopping wars are temporary, lack substance, "cosplay" rather than real diplomacy

**Examples**:
- **Armenia-Azerbaijan**: Temporary, not meaningful reconciliation
- **Gaza**: "Deal" in NYC real estate sense (common goal, figure out details later), already falling apart. Hamas explicitly said they didn't agree to disarm part of "deal"
- **Afghanistan parallel**: Trump talked to Taliban, not Afghan government. Vague deal with no checks. Timed withdrawal after he left office - Biden got "egg on his face" from incompetent deal.

**Mike's warning**: "Let's wait 5 years and look back. No diplomat would claim what Hamas half-agreed to is a final deal. Gaza deal may fail like Afghanistan."

### Left-Right Narrative Divide

**Al Chang's insight** (Jerry's friend):

1. There's a set of facts on the ground
2. Left and right see roughly the same facts
3. But have **completely different movie scripts/narratives** around them
4. Both narratives are relatively consistent and work
5. Two sides **can't see or read each other's narratives** - incapable

**Jerry's question**: "How do I steel man the opposing narrative? How do I put my brain into it and listen for different parts?"

**Jerry's method**: Asked Claude and ChatGPT: "What has Trump actually done?" Discovered his people have been on the ground doing stuff that hasn't been done before in multiple places.

**Pattern**: Same facts, different interpretive frameworks, mutual incomprehension.
