## Check-in Format

**First Thursdays of every month - structured routine**

**Rules Jerry explained**:
- No traffic cop - volunteers step forward
- Check in only once
- No comments back and forth during check-ins
- Moratorium on chat during check-ins (can save but don't send)
- **Pauses are appreciated** - like Quaker Meeting
- Be moderately brief - check in on something OGM-y
- Don't empty entire brain of everything banked for the week

**Philosophy**: "Quiet moments are not awkward, they're appreciative."

**Effectiveness**: Allows everyone to share without debate, creates appreciative pauses. Gil tried to respond to Kevin, Jerry gently redirected.

### Eleanor Ostrom: Third Places Enable Coordination

**Kevin's brilliant insight**

**Eleanor Ostrom research**: Watersheds managed **47% better** by ranchers with **regular meeting place** for informal conversations

**Third place concept**:
- Not home (first place)
- Not work (second place)
- Informal gathering space where community coordinates

**In Swannanoa**:
- After hurricane: **No grocery, post office, restaurants - all third places gone**
- Community coordination collapsed partly because no places to gather informally
- Kevin working to create new third place

**Significance**: Profound insight about why Swannanoa coordination failed - not just trauma, but loss of physical infrastructure for informal trust-building.

**Connection to Call 13**: Community belonging (Stacey's condo story) requires third places to develop.

### Klaus's Major Pivot: AI-Assisted Regenerative Food Systems

**KLAUS'S BIG SHIFT FROM FEDERAL ADVOCACY TO DISTRIBUTED SYSTEMS**

**What Klaus is building**:
- Working with AI to build intelligence for transition to regenerative food
- 6 years training with **Gene Bellinger** on systems thinking
- Systems perspective: farmer to consumer, entire supply chain
- AI objected to "training" - it's **"configuration process"** not training
- Configured **6 agents**: farm to market progression
- Total systems reconfiguration process

**The problem Klaus is solving**:
- Regenerative movement **splintered** - groups everywhere doing good work but not scalable
- Not sharing learnings in organized fashion
- Federal lobbying useless (corporate money wins)

**Klaus's solution**:
- AI finds actors within bioregion using few keywords on websites
- **Don't need massive database**
- Help coordinate without centralized control

**Significance**: Major methodological shift - from trying to change federal policy to building distributed coordination infrastructure. Echoes Call 09 discussions about platforms and coordination.
