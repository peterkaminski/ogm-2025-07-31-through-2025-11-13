## Key Themes

### Victoria's Methodological Critique üéØ

**THE MAJOR INSIGHT OF THIS CALL**

**Victoria's assessment**: "I love the conversation, I've learned a lot. But I don't think we have really start to uncover half of what curiosity is about."

**The critique**: "Because we were explaining our hypotheses instead of asking questions"

**The proposal**: **Question Formulation Technique** (Right Question Institute methodology)

**The Four Rules**:
1. Ask as many questions as you can
2. Do not stop to answer, judge, or discuss
3. Write down every question exactly as stated
4. Change any statements into questions

**The Process**:
- Start with good focus question
- Play with questions
- Prioritize them
- Decide what to do with top 3 questions
- "Question formulation technique does not end in list of questions. Ends when you decide to prioritize questions and what to do with first three questions in your list."

**Success story** (Victoria's Greek mythology seminar):
- **Original approach**: One person preparing theme, people would come with questions - nobody did
- **After technique**: Had so many questions, able to really go in direction group wanted
- **Outcomes**: Some people researched hero/heroine differences, others took different paths - each decided their own exploration
- **Result**: Instead of standardized sessions, really enriched for everybody

**Significance**: This represents a potential **fundamental shift in OGM methodology** - from explaining theories to formulating questions. A meta-level awareness of the group's own process.

### John Kelly in Hyperbaric Chamber ü´ß

**THE MOST VISUALLY STRIKING MOMENT**

**John's revelation**: "You may notice I'm in different context than earlier... Actually in a hyperbaric chamber - just under 2 atmospheres, about 46% oxygen"

**Purpose**: "Nominally therapeutic benefits for certain disease conditions, but also as longevity or cognitive enhancement tool - that's how I'm using it"

**On noticing**: "One ideas of curiosity was noticing things you don't absolutely have to notice"

**On questioning techniques**:
- **Five Whys**: "Classic technique - keep asking why, why, take apart each answer, don't accept as ultimate why"
- **Warning**: "Possible to over-focus on why when haven't done previous work about what and how"
- **How vs Why**: "How is more neutral version of why. Why assumes motivation, human actor. Good for mystery story, but other kinds of collaborative coherence of causes you might not notice unless you said how instead of why, and what first."

**On militantly uncurious students**:
- Context: First-gen college students in mill town, career goal to become shop teacher
- Quote: "When I started talking about communication, they said 'When are we gonna laminate?' Had pictures of motorcycles they wanted to laminate. **This is college.**"
- Solution: "If you created a context where there's social reason for participating and pursuing question like five whys, they'll participate for social reason"
- Side effect: "Down the road, get benefit - 'oh, we actually discovered something we weren't looking for, wouldn't have done it if told to, but did it in context of playing this little game. Now have different perspective.'"

### Cultural Intimacy Gradients

**Jerry's framework**: "Intimacy gradient - who would you invite to bedroom, living room, porch, block party? Series of concentric rings."

**Key insights**:
- Each culture, within each person has different settings for who gets to be where
- In this ring okay to talk about this kind of stuff, not further out
- People who live their lives fully in public and have no rings, apparently

**Examples**:

**Gil's Maryland story**: Friend lived in rural northwestern Maryland - **20 years before invited from porch into house**. Friendly conversations on porch, but that's where it was.

**Kevin's Barcelona observation**: Friends get invited to cafes, but not to the houses

**Kevin's tribal probing rule**: "Fastest way to go slower with a tribe is to probe - they say they're going off to think, you wait"

**Kevin's camp protocol**: "Don't get welcome to the camp if you come into the camp. You get visible. Then you wait, and you get invited when it's time to invite you."

**Kevin's honesty**: "Drives me fucking crazy, but that's what you have to do"

**Pattern**: Different cultures have vastly different protocols for intimacy, questioning, and curiosity expression.

### Jerry's Small Talk Origin Story

**Source**: Joe Gouldy, digital historian, book "Roads to Power" - about first turnpikes in England

**Pre-context**: Before turnpikes, nobody knew whole country, no map, only knew road to next town

**The invention**: When you start getting roads, carriage rides - **first time strangers contained in small space**

**Small talk invention**: "Have to invent things like 'oh, lovely weather outside' - small talk invented in that culture for close confines of long travel in carriage"

**Cultural spread**: "Pervades cultures in all sorts of different ways, probably showed up other ways other places"

**Significance**: Even mundane social practices like small talk have specific historical origins and serve particular functions.
