# Call 01: Grief and Societal Trauma

**Date**: July 31, 2025
**Type**: Hybrid (check-in elements + topical discussion)
**Duration**: ~98 minutes
**Facilitator**: Jerry Michalski

---

## Quick Summary

The first OGM call in this series tackled how societies process collective trauma versus individual grief. Klaus Mager shared vulnerable stories of growing up in post-WWII Germany, Kevin Jones revealed the ongoing devastation in Swannanoa after Hurricane Helene, and Stacey Druss delivered powerful testimony on systemic male violence and institutional complicity. The call established foundational themes that would recur throughout the series, particularly Kevin's story arc of community trauma.

## Key Themes

### Societal Grief

**How societies process collective trauma vs. individual grief**

Examples discussed:
- German post-WWII trauma and guilt
- Pinochet's Chile
- Argentina's Dirty War
- Gaza/Israel conflict
- Swannanoa flood (Hurricane Helene)
- 9/11 attacks

The group explored the question: How do societies process grief well versus poorly? Documentary evidence (like Matthew Brady's Civil War photos) and truth & reconciliation processes emerged as two approaches, though their effectiveness was debated.

---

### Intergenerational Trauma

**Trauma passed through generations**

**Klaus Mager's powerful sharing**: Born in 1949, exposed to concentration camp footage as a young child in Germany, witnessed his parents' generation's defensiveness and rationalization.

**Key insights**:
- Klaus: Watching concentration camp footage as a child shaped his understanding of collective guilt
- Generational defensiveness vs. shame
- Holocaust survivors' trauma transmission to children and grandchildren
- Palestinian/Israeli generational wounds perpetuating conflict

---

### Climate Disasters and Community Resilience

**Community collapse after natural disasters**

**Kevin Jones's story** (which becomes a major arc across the entire call series):

Kevin shared the devastating aftermath of Hurricane Helene in Swannanoa:
- Fled to Europe after the disaster
- Community leadership exhaustion
- FEMA inadequacy
- Property disputes with his own children
- Planning to move to Asheville, can't bear staying in Swannanoa

Klaus compared this to Oregon wildfire recovery, noting that Swannanoa seemed worse due to lack of federal support.

This is the **beginning of Kevin's story arc** that will continue through Call 11.

---

### Gender-Based Violence

**Systemic male violence and institutional complicity**

**Stacey Druss's powerful testimony**:
- 80%+ of violent crimes are perpetrated by men
- Child trafficking and abuse networks
- Institutions protect perpetrators
- Cultural silence and complicity
- **Call to action**: Need for men to speak out in their circles

Stacey shared two personal stories:
1. Her marriage to a "fight to win" husband whose win-at-all-costs mentality "killed the family"
2. Childhood memory of a boy suffocating frogs - male violence normalized from early age

She noted being the only woman on the call made her statement harder to deliver.

---

### Grief Processing Approaches

**How to process grief well vs. poorly**

Approaches discussed:
- **Telling stories**: Jerry noted his family history was lost, emphasizing importance of preserving narratives
- **Truth and reconciliation**: Argentina's "Nunca Mas" report, South Africa's process, Canada's residential schools
- **Documentary evidence**: Matthew Brady's Civil War photography
- **Jewish tradition**: Eric Rangell explained acknowledging grief in all life events - "always bittersweet, never perfect happiness (oy)", Tisha B'Av fast day
- **Artistic community building**: Marshall High Studios - artist collective rebuilding Marshall, NC after Hurricane Helene

## Frameworks Introduced

### Truth and Reconciliation

**Context**: Argentina, South Africa, Canada

**Discussed by**: Jerry Michalski, Gil Friend, Klaus Mager

**Effectiveness**: Mixed - helps acknowledgment but doesn't always solve underlying issues. Argentina's "Nunca Mas" (Never Again) report documented disappeared persons. South Africa's process post-apartheid. Canada's residential schools reckoning.

### Jewish Grief Tradition

**Introduced by**: Eric Rangell

**Key point**: Always bittersweet, never perfect happiness (oy). Tisha B'Av fast day. Acknowledging grief throughout life cycle.

### 3 C's of Processing

**Elements**: Choose, Connect, Communicate

**Mentioned by**: Stacey Druss
